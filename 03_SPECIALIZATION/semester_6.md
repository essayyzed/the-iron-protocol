### Technical Interview Prep (Weeks 11-14)

**Data Structures & Algorithms Review:**

Weekly Problem Solving Schedule:
```
Monday:     2 Array/String problems
Tuesday:    2 Linked List/Stack/Queue problems
Wednesday:  2 Tree/Graph problems
Thursday:   2 Dynamic Programming problems
Friday:     Mock interview (1 hour)
Saturday:   Review mistakes
Sunday:     System design study
```

**Problem Solving Approach (UMPIRE):**

1. **Understand:** Clarify the problem
2. **Match:** Identify patterns
3. **Plan:** Outline approach
4. **Implement:** Write code
5. **Review:** Check edge cases
6. **Evaluate:** Analyze complexity

**Essential Patterns to Master:**
- Two Pointers
- Sliding Window
- Fast & Slow Pointers
- Merge Intervals
- Cyclic Sort
- In-place Reversal of Linked List
- Tree BFS/DFS
- Binary Search
- Top K Elements
- K-way Merge
- Dynamic Programming patterns

**System Design Basics:**

For internship-level interviews, focus on:
- Designing a URL shortener
- Designing a simple cache
- Designing a rate limiter
- Designing a messaging system
- Load balancing basics
- Database scaling (vertical vs horizontal)
- Caching strategies

**Study Resources:**
- [Grokking the Coding Interview](https://www.educative.io/courses/grokking-the-coding-interview)
- [NeetCode 150](https://neetcode.io/)
- [System Design Primer](https://github.com/donnemartin/system-design-primer)

### Behavioral Interview Prep (Week 15)

**STAR Method Template:**

For each project/experience, prepare stories:
- **Situation:** Context and background
- **Task:** Your responsibility
- **Action:** What you did specifically
- **Result:** Outcome and learnings

**Common Questions:**
1. Tell me about yourself
2. Why this company/role?
3. Tell me about a challenging project
4. How do you handle disagreements?
5. Describe a time you failed
6. Where do you see yourself in 5 years?

**Your Stories Bank:**

Prepare 5-7 stories covering:
- Technical challenge you overcame
- Leadership or initiative
- Collaboration and teamwork
- Failure and learning
- Time management under pressure

## üéì Final Year Project (FYP) Planning Sprint

**Why now?** Your FYP proposal is likely due in **Semester 7 (Week 1-4)**. Planning now gives you:
- Time to refine your idea
- Summer Break 3 for preliminary work/research
- First pick of supervisors before they're overbooked
- Reduced stress in Year 4

### Weeks 11-12: FYP Domain Selection

**Step 1: Choose Your Domain**

Based on your Semester 5 specialization:
- Web/Backend ‚Üí Real-time systems, scalability, microservices
- ML/AI ‚Üí Computer vision, NLP, recommendation systems
- Security ‚Üí Penetration testing tools, cryptography, secure systems
- Mobile ‚Üí Cross-platform frameworks, native performance
- Data ‚Üí Analytics dashboards, ETL pipelines, data visualization

**Step 2: Problem Identification**

```markdown
## FYP Ideas Template

### Idea 1: [Project Name]
**Problem:** What problem does this solve?
**Users:** Who will use this?
**Novelty:** What makes it different from existing solutions?
**Feasibility:** Can I build this in 6-8 months?
**Tech Stack:** What technologies will I use?
**Learning:** What will I learn from this?
```

**Generate 3-5 ideas**, then narrow to 1-2 strongest.

### Weeks 13-14: Supervisor Search & Proposal Draft

**Finding a Supervisor:**

1. **Research faculty interests:**
   - Check department website for research areas
   - Read recent publications
   - Look at past FYPs they supervised

2. **Make the approach:**
   ```
   Subject: FYP Supervision Request - [Your Name]
   
   Dear Dr. [Name],
   
   I am a [Year] student specializing in [area] and planning my FYP.
   I'm interested in [specific topic related to their research].
   
   My idea: [2-3 sentence pitch]
   
   Would you be available to supervise? I have:
   - [Relevant coursework]
   - [Relevant projects/skills]
   - [Any preliminary research done]
   
   I can meet at your convenience to discuss.
   
   Best regards,
   [Your Name]
   ```

3. **Have 2-3 backup options** in case first choice is unavailable.

**Preliminary Proposal (1-2 pages):**

```markdown
# FYP Proposal Draft

## Title
[Project Name]

## Problem Statement
What problem exists? Who faces it? Why does it matter?

## Objectives
1. Primary objective
2. Secondary objective
3. Stretch goal (optional)

## Proposed Solution
High-level approach to solving the problem.

## Technology Stack
- Frontend: [if applicable]
- Backend: [if applicable]
- Database: [if applicable]
- ML/AI tools: [if applicable]
- Deployment: [if applicable]

## Timeline (Rough)
- Semester 7: Research, design, core implementation
- Semester 8: Testing, optimization, documentation

## Expected Deliverables
- Working software/system
- Documentation (technical + user manual)
- Research paper/report (if research-oriented)

## References
[3-5 key papers or projects that inspired this]
```
